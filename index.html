About Embracing Memoir

Hugging is one of the most genuine forms of human connection. Is a machine capable of embracing? Embracing Memoir is a project for decoding and coding hugs.

The goal is to make a wearable able to register hugs through sensors and teach it to embrace through machine learning and pneumatic actuators.

The piece acts like a Memoir, a collection of hugs that we carry throughout life. It is a garment capable of rescuing memories and interacting affectively with humans.

1st

Hugging a machine

The device is a jacket containing pressure sensors that detect the touch points and intensity of the touch on the various parts of the body during a hug. The capabilities of the fabric to incorporate these sensors through conductive lines or embroidery will be explored. The data will be sent to a digital database, where a touch map of hundreds of hugs will be made.

2nd

Machine Learning

This information will be cross-referenced with users' cultural and emotional information in order to investigate the characteristics that define different types of hugs. I want to get in touch with researchers in neurosciences and affective computing around the world to enrich this data. This research will be the basis for the construction of an algorithm that teaches the jacket to embrace and identify a hug.

3rd

Hugging machine

Is it possible for the machine to understand whether a hug is more affective or more invasive? Embracing Memoir wants to understand the language of a hug through data. The final phase of the project will be the jacket's performance in embracing the user. A pneumatic system combined with soft robotics can control the movement of the cloth and reproduce the application of pressure at specific points during a certain time. In addition, the jacket will be able to control its own temperature using thermoelectric modules. With pressure, temperature and time I want to reproduce a human embrace through a machine.

In addition to its artistic dimension, the project consists of an unprecedented research that provides data on human behavior through wearables. It also has the potential to open paths in the area of therapeutic wearables that act by pressing specific parts of the body for treatment purposes.

I also have the desire to evolve this project by making biometric measurements in the future such as heart rate, sweating and body position during the hug, to identify biological responses to the hugs and add even more information dimensions in the algorithm.

Just like the skin, which touches and is touched, clothing can be a new type of skin, which feels and acts. Embracing Memoir is coming to build this new paradigm on wearables.
